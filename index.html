Innovations by the Faculty in Teaching and Learning

Faculty members of the Department of Electronics and Communication Engineering at Dhanekula Institute of Engineering and Technology have proactively embraced and implemented a wide range of innovative teaching-learning strategies. These innovations are closely aligned with Outcome-Based Education (OBE) practices and aim to improve Course Outcome (CO) and Program Outcome (PO) attainments, bridge curriculum-industry gaps, and promote holistic student development.



1. Peer-Assisted Learning (PAL):

Objective: Foster cooperative learning through mentorship.

Implementation: Advanced learners identified via Class Test-1 guide peers by solving and explaining problems in class.

Impact: Improves conceptual understanding, communication skills, and peer collaboration.

2. Research-Oriented Projects & Discussions:

Approach: Students explore current research topics through mini and major projects; faculty guide literature review, research framing, and experimentation.

Result: Multiple publications in reputed journals and student-led conference papers.

View Research Project Details

3. Virtual Labs and Simulation Tools:

Tools Used: NPTEL Virtual Labs, MATLAB, Proteus, Multisim.

Courses Mapped: DSP, Digital Circuits, Communication Systems.

Benefit: Enables remote learning and visualization of abstract concepts.

Access Virtual Lab Resources

4. Seminar-Driven Learning:

Practice: Each student delivers a seminar on emerging topics.

Faculty Role: Guide content preparation, conduct Q&A sessions, assess presentation skills.

Outcome: Enhances research aptitude, technical communication, and domain awareness.

5. Collaborative Projects and Annual Project Expo (ISF):

Method: Group projects across interdisciplinary domains showcased at DIET-ISF Project Expo.

Skills Enhanced: Teamwork, communication, prototyping.

View Project Expo Details

6. Problem-Based Learning (PBL) and Hackathons:

Example: ML Hackathon for ECE students in Communication Domain (2024).

Approach: Identify real-world problems and develop solutions using ML.

Result: Increased PO1, PO2, PO3, PO5 attainment.

ML Hackathon Files

7. Flipped Classroom Using AI Tools:

Process: Pre-class videos, AI tools for concept delivery, in-class application-based discussion.

Courses Implemented: ECA, EMTL

Access Teaching Materials

8. Cooperative Learning with Social Impact Projects:

Context: Final-year students undertake community-based projects under faculty mentorship.

Examples: IoT and Drone related products as given in table below.

Outcome: PO6, PO7, and PO8 enhancement.
